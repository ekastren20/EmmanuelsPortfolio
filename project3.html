Art-Teaching Application

Prototype for an app that teaches the fundamentals of art creation.

Project Overview

Duration:

2-weeks

Role:

UX/UI Designer & Team Manager.

Team:

2-weeks

Tools:

Figma, Trello, Slack, Zoom

The Problem

There is a lack art education apps to help with enhancing people's artistic skills. While there are plenty of websites to subscribe to, there isn't an application to pass the time with that would ultimately benefit ones artistic ability and knowledge.

The Solution

My team and I developed a prototype which is a lesson based app that provides professional videos and personalized feedback from industry professionals. Artists are also provided a community to display their work and connect with other artists. The app is designed to have the user sign up for a subscription at low cost, since teachers will be included as well. See below for the main prototype presentation.

User Research Interviews

In this research project, we planned to do 1-2 interviews each between the four of us. Interviews on average were 15 minutes. Some useful quotes from our interviews are provided below.

"Being able to ask questions to direct help and customer service. Being able to connect with artists and teachers directly”

“A community, that I miss the most. Right now, I'm seeking a community and I can't find it.”

“Never look at it as a time frame, Look at it as a lifestyle commitment- - your whole life. Every chance you get you have to practice. Even the best artists still practice.

Affinity Diagram & Empathy Map

We used our interview answers to create sticky notes in Figma, of which were organized into an Affinity Diagram and then an Empathy Map.

User Persona

After gathering our responses from our 7 interviewees we concluded that our user persona would be around 31 yrs old, a hair stylist residing in Orlando, has a liberal arts degree and is single. Interests include; art and film, animation, fashion and style. Influences are work, family, artists and music She is motivated by having an art community, downtime and improving her art skills. Pain points are lack of time, ineptitude and having no incentives.

Mind Map & Wireframing

Initial “Mind Map” and User flow from Login. This did not end up being our final User Flow as used in the prototype. One key change was signing up as a new member instead of logging in. The Mind Map helped us figure out the different branches the user should go after the Login and Home Page. Something left out of the prototype but included in this Mind Map is the option to choose International vs. Local Community Boards.

Challenges: User Flow, Components, Consistency

User Testing & Iterations

While conducting user tests, and from our own observations, we found that several key changes were necessary. For example, having a clear and distinct payment plan for users, displaying a labeled icon bar, and consistent patterns and visuals that run across all pages.

Conclusion

Initially, the app was a challenge to figure out. We wanted an app based on enhancing artistic ability, but how to go about that gave room to a good amount of brainstorming. We set our focus on creating a lesson-based app with a membership program, offering perks, certificates and an art community. Using similar apps as a guideline like DuoLingo, a successful language-learning app, we were able to derive a blueprint for the wireframing. Wireframing presented its own challenges. Creating a feasible user flow was key, though difficult, since a variety pages hold many navigation options. Connecting pages properly set up scenarios where duplicate pages, with minor changes, had to be created. Focus on consistency and usage of components was key in making the app uniform throughout. Once a proper flow was created with uniformity it was very rewarding.
