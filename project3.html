Art-Teaching Application

Prototype for an app that teaches the fundamentals of art creation.

2-weeks

UX/UI Designer

3-people

Figma/Trello/Slack/InVision/Excel

The Problem

There is a lack of art education apps to help with enhancing people's artistic skills. While there are plenty of websites to subscribe to, there isn't an application to pass the time with that would ultimately benefit ones artistic ability and knowledge.

Step 1: User Research Interviews

In this research project, we planned to do 1-2 interviews each between the four of us. Interviews on average were 15 minutes. Some useful quotes from our interviews are provided below.

"Being able to ask questions to direct help and customer service. Being able to connect with artists and teachers directly”

“A community, that I miss the most. Right now, I'm seeking a community and I can't find it.”

“Never look at it as a time frame, Look at it as a lifestyle commitment- - your whole life. Every chance you get you have to practice. Even the best artists still practice."

Step 2: User Persona

After gathering our responses from our 7 interviewees we concluded that our user persona would be around 31 yrs old, a hair stylist residing in Orlando, has a liberal arts degree and is single. Interests include; art and film, animation, fashion and style. Influences are work, family, artists and music She is motivated by having an art community, downtime and improving her art skills. Pain points are lack of time, ineptitude and having no incentives.

Step 3: Mind Map

Initial “Mind Map” and User flow from Login. This did not end up being our final User Flow as used in the prototype. One key change was signing up as a new member instead of logging in. The Mind Map helped us figure out the different branches the user should go after the Login and Home Page. Something left out of the prototype but included in this Mind Map is the option to choose International vs. Local Community Boards.

Step 4: User Flow

One of our major challenges was establishing a User Flow. How would someone navigate our app under various circumstances, such as being a first time user vs. someone with a well established account?

Step 5: User Testing & Iterations

While conducting user tests, and from our own observations, we found that several key changes were necessary. For example, having a clear and distinct payment plan for users, displaying a labeled icon bar, and consistent patterns and visuals that run across all pages.

Step 6

This step was all about creating a theme. After creating individual mood boards, we came together as a team and created a UI style tile containing suggested color schemes, typography, icons, photos, and button appearances.

Step 7

Now it was time to upgrade our wireframe to include our newly found theme. In our mid-fidelity prototype, we added our main "money green" theme to the nav bar and drop-down menu, as well as to our Treasury Direct logo. We also established image placeholders and cemented the footer with a grey background and list-like display of items.

Step 8

We conducted user testing to see how our page impressed so far.

Conclusion

In our usability tests of the original site, users were overwhelmed by the amount of information on the homepage. In our redesign of the homepage, we kept much of the information from the original but we organized it in a more simplified way. The fact that users in our last test felt less overwhelmed indicates that organization and design plays a major role is users reactions to being presented with lots of information. One factor that did not change between tests is the users comfortability with the subject matter. The lack of knowledge about investing in securities made users still slightly uncomfortable navigating the site. Throughout this project, our team worked hard to organize the extensive content on the site in a more simplified way. All the while, struggling with the subject matter as well. However, our final user tests revealed we accomplished our goal in redesigning our site to make it look like a user-friendly, legitimate government site.
